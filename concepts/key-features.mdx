## Goal tracking

Goal tracking is the heart of Trxphy. You create a goal by giving it a title, an optional description, and a target date, then choose a progress measurement style — either a simple percentage you update manually or a numeric value that Trxphy calculates from your check-ins. Every update you make is timestamped and stored, building a complete history of how the goal evolved from its first day to its last.

## Milestones

Milestones let you carve a goal into concrete, time-bound steps. Each milestone carries its own due date and a completion toggle, and Trxphy rolls up milestone completion into your overall goal progress automatically. When a milestone is overdue, the dashboard highlights it so you can take action before the parent goal falls off track.

## Progress analytics

Progress analytics turn your check-in history into actionable insight. The built-in charts show you how your progress percentage has changed over time, overlay your milestone completion events, and compare your actual pace against the trajectory needed to hit your target date. You can filter by goal, date range, or tag to zero in on exactly the data you need.

## Integrations

Trxphy connects to external tools through the Integrations settings page. Once you authorize a connection, Trxphy can sync events, tasks, or data points from those tools directly into your goals and milestones. See the [Integrations guide](/guides/integrations) for the full list of supported connections and setup steps.

## Team collaboration

Team workspaces let every member view and contribute to the same goals. You can leave comments on individual milestones, @-mention teammates, and assign a goal owner so it is always clear who is responsible for a given outcome. Admins manage membership and permission levels from the workspace settings page.

## Notifications

Trxphy sends notifications when milestones are approaching, when a goal falls behind its target pace, and on a weekly digest schedule. You control the frequency and channel — email, in-app, or both — from your [notification settings](/guides/notifications).
