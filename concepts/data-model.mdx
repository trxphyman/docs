Every piece of data in Trxphy has a well-defined shape and a clear relationship to the rest of the system. Understanding the data model helps you design your goals thoughtfully, use the API confidently, and get the most out of filters, analytics, and integrations. The four primary building blocks are goals, milestones, check-ins, and tags.

## Goals

A goal is the top-level object in Trxphy. It represents a single outcome you want to achieve and acts as the parent for all milestones and check-ins beneath it.

| Field | Type | Description |
|---|---|---|
| `title` | string | A short, human-readable name for the goal. Required. |
| `description` | string | An optional longer explanation of what the goal involves and why it matters. |
| `target_date` | date | The date by which you intend to complete the goal. Used to calculate pacing in analytics. |
| `status` | enum | Current lifecycle state: `draft`, `active`, or `complete`. |
| `progress` | integer (0–100) | A percentage representing how close the goal is to completion. Updated manually or derived from check-ins. |

## Milestones

A milestone is a time-bound checkpoint that belongs to exactly one goal. Completing milestones advances your goal's overall progress and keeps long-running work from stalling.

| Field | Type | Description |
|---|---|---|
| `title` | string | A concise description of what needs to be done. Required. |
| `due_date` | date | The date by which this milestone should be completed. |
| `completed` | boolean | Whether the milestone has been marked as done. Defaults to `false`. |

## Check-ins

A check-in is a timestamped record of progress against a goal. Each one captures exactly where you stood on a specific date, and together they build the historical data that powers your analytics charts.

A check-in carries three fields:

- **date** — The calendar date the check-in is recorded for. Trxphy defaults to today but you can back-date entries.
- **note** — A freeform text field where you describe what you accomplished, what you learned, or any blockers you faced.
- **value** — A numeric value representing your progress at the time of the check-in. For percentage-based goals this is a number between 0 and 100; for numeric goals it reflects your raw measurement (steps walked, pages written, revenue earned, and so on).

## Tags

Tags are short, freeform labels you attach to goals to make filtering and organization easier. A goal can carry as many tags as you like, and tags are shared across your workspace so your whole team works from the same vocabulary.

Use tags to group goals by theme (`health`, `q3-launch`, `learning`), by owner, by project, or by any other dimension that fits your workflow. The dashboard filter bar and the analytics view both let you narrow results to a specific tag or combination of tags in a single click.

<Info>
  All goal data — including milestones, check-ins, and tags — is **private by default**. Your data is only visible to other people when you explicitly move a goal into a shared team workspace or grant a teammate direct access. See the [First Steps guide](/guides/first-steps) to learn how to share goals with your team.
</Info>
