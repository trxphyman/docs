Trxphy gives you meaningful control over how your workspace looks and feels. Whether you prefer a minimal dark interface late at night or a bright, information-dense layout during your morning planning session, you can configure the dashboard to match the way you work.

## Themes

Choose the color theme that suits your environment. Navigate to **Settings → Appearance** to find the theme selector, then pick from the three options below.

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Light">
    The Light theme uses a white background with dark text and high-contrast UI elements. It works well in brightly lit offices and on screens where ambient glare is a factor.

    To activate it, open **Settings → Appearance**, select **Light** under the Theme section, and click **Save**. The interface updates immediately without requiring a page reload.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Dark">
    The Dark theme uses a deep charcoal background with light text, reducing eye strain during evening work sessions and in low-light environments.

    To activate it, open **Settings → Appearance**, select **Dark** under the Theme section, and click **Save**. All charts and progress visualizations automatically switch to dark-optimized color palettes.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="System">
    The System theme mirrors your operating system's current appearance setting. When your OS switches from light to dark mode at sunset — or when you toggle it manually — Trxphy follows automatically.

    To activate it, open **Settings → Appearance**, select **System** under the Theme section, and click **Save**. No further configuration is needed; Trxphy reads the `prefers-color-scheme` signal from your browser.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Dashboard layout

Your dashboard is made up of widgets — cards that display your active goals, upcoming milestones, recent check-ins, and summary statistics. You can rearrange these widgets to put the information you care about most at the top.

To **reorder widgets**, click and hold the drag handle (the six-dot icon) in the top-left corner of any widget, then drag it to a new position in the grid. Release to drop it in place. The layout saves automatically.

To **hide or show sections**, click **Customize layout** in the top-right corner of the dashboard. A panel opens listing every available widget with a visibility toggle next to each one. Turn off any widget you don't need — it disappears from your dashboard but can be re-enabled at any time.

Trxphy also supports two **density modes** for the goal list:

- **Grid view** — displays goals as cards arranged in columns, showing the goal title, progress bar, and next milestone at a glance. Best for dashboards with fewer than ten active goals.
- **List view** — displays goals in a compact vertical list with more detail per row, including the last check-in date and tag labels. Best for power users managing many goals simultaneously.

Switch between grid and list view using the toggle in the top-right corner of the goal section.

## Goal display options

Fine-tune how goals are ordered and grouped on your dashboard to surface the most relevant information at any moment.

**Sort order** — click the **Sort** dropdown above the goal list and choose from:

- **By date** — orders goals by target date, with the soonest deadline at the top
- **By priority** — orders goals by the priority level (High, Medium, Low) you assigned at creation
- **By progress** — orders goals by completion percentage, so you can focus on those falling behind

**Filter by tag** — click the **Filter** button and select one or more tags to show only goals that carry those labels. Active filters appear as chips above the goal list and can be removed individually with a single click.

**Group by status** — enable **Group by status** from the Sort & Filter panel to split your goal list into three sections: Active, On Hold, and Completed. This keeps finished goals visible for reference without mixing them into your working list.

## Custom goal colors

Assign a color to any goal to create an instant visual identity for it across your dashboard, charts, and calendar sync. Colors help you distinguish goals at a glance without reading titles, especially useful when you have many goals running in parallel.

To assign a color, open a goal, click the colored circle next to the goal title, and pick from the 12 available palette options. The color applies immediately and appears on the goal's card in the dashboard, on milestone markers in the Analytics chart, and on synced Google Calendar events.

To remove a custom color and return to the default gray, open the same color picker and click **Reset to default**.

<Tip>
  Theme and layout preferences are specific to your account, not your device. If you also want to update your avatar, display name, or timezone, head over to [Profile settings](/settings/profile) — those details appear alongside your goals in shared reports and team views.
</Tip>
