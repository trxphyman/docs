Keeping your Trxphy account secure protects your goals, personal data, and any team workspaces you belong to. Trxphy gives you direct control over your password, two-factor authentication, and the devices that have active access to your account — all from a single Security settings page.

## Change your password

You should update your password if you suspect it has been compromised or if you have not changed it in a while. Trxphy requires your current password before setting a new one, so no one else can change it without your knowledge.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Security Settings">
    Navigate to **Settings → Security** from the left sidebar.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select Change Password">
    Click **Change Password** in the Password section.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter your current password">
    Type your existing password in the **Current password** field to verify your identity.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter your new password">
    Type your chosen password in the **New password** field. Use at least 12 characters with a mix of letters, numbers, and symbols for best security.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm and save">
    Re-enter the new password in the **Confirm new password** field, then click **Save Password**. You will receive a confirmation email to your registered address.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Two-factor authentication

Two-factor authentication (2FA) adds a second layer of protection by requiring a time-based one-time code from an authenticator app — such as Google Authenticator, Authy, or 1Password — every time you sign in.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Security Settings">
    Navigate to **Settings → Security** from the left sidebar.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enable 2FA">
    Click **Enable Two-Factor Authentication** in the 2FA section. Trxphy displays a QR code and a manual setup key.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Scan the QR code">
    Open your authenticator app, tap **Add account** (or the **+** icon), and scan the QR code displayed on screen. If your app does not support QR scanning, enter the manual setup key instead.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter the verification code">
    Your authenticator app generates a six-digit code. Enter it in the **Verification code** field on Trxphy and click **Verify**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save your backup codes">
    Trxphy displays a set of single-use backup codes. Copy them and store them somewhere safe — for example in a password manager or a printed document kept offline.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
  Your backup codes are shown **only once**. If you lose both your authenticator device and your backup codes, you will be locked out of your account. Store them securely before closing this screen. You can generate a new set of backup codes from **Settings → Security → Backup Codes** at any time, which immediately invalidates the previous set.
</Warning>

## Active sessions

Every time you sign in to Trxphy — from a browser, desktop app, or mobile device — a new session is created. You can see all active sessions and revoke any that you do not recognise.

To manage your sessions, go to **Settings → Security → Active Sessions**. Each session entry shows the device type, browser or app name, approximate location, and the time of last activity. To end a specific session, click **Revoke** next to it. To sign out of every other device at once, click **Revoke All Other Sessions**.

Revoking a session immediately signs that device out. The next person who tries to use Trxphy on that device will need to sign in again.

## Account recovery

If you lose access to your account, Trxphy offers two recovery paths:

- **Email-based recovery** — On the sign-in page, click **Forgot password?** and enter your registered email address. Trxphy sends a secure reset link valid for 30 minutes.
- **Backup codes** — If 2FA is enabled and you cannot access your authenticator app, enter one of your saved backup codes on the 2FA prompt screen to complete sign-in and regain access.

If neither option works — for example, you have also lost access to your email address — contact the Trxphy support team to begin a manual identity verification process.

<Note>
  If you notice sign-in attempts or account changes you did not make, report the suspicious activity to the Trxphy team immediately via the [Contact](/help/contact) page so we can help you secure your account.
</Note>
